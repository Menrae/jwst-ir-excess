feat: set single-band significance threshold, add qc_single_band_candidate

Resolved as a near-prerequisite before any larger run, after the 15-field
trial showed 270/273 (98.9%) of real stars are single-band-only --
qc_candidate_preliminary requires both bands, so until this was set the
pipeline could only ever produce a candidate from the ~1% dual-band
minority of what real archive data actually looks like.

config/pipeline_config.yaml, pipeline/excess.py:
- Literature check first: neither Carrigan (2009) nor Griffith et al.
  (2015) offers a directly borrowable number. Carrigan's own resolution
  to an analogous 2-filter problem was to avoid a 2-point significance
  statistic entirely, adding LRS spectroscopy specifically because "the
  associated impossibility of fitting a Planck distribution with just
  two filter points" made a meaningful statistic impossible with only
  two. Griffith's method is structurally multi-band (WISE colors), with
  no single-band tier to compare against at all
- Derived a value instead: the dual-band criterion's own credibility
  comes from requiring both bands to independently clear 3 sigma: under
  a rough independence approximation (one-tailed P~1.35e-3 each), the
  joint probability is ~1.8e-6, and the single one-tailed z giving that
  same tail probability alone is ~4.7. Rounded up to 5.0, not down,
  given known F770W/F1000W flux-error correlation (shared PSF-fit
  method, shared diffuse-background-annulus systematic) that the
  independence approximation ignores
- Verified the proposed value against real data before proposing it:
  in the 273-star, 15-field trial, only 2/273 single-band stars even
  reach a computed excess_sigma at all (the rest disqualified earlier,
  chiefly qc_poor_photosphere_fit); the one large value found (sigma=
  410.8) is already disqualified by qc_poor_photosphere_fit and
  qc_rj_extrapolated regardless -- this value was known to change zero
  current results before implementation, not just assumed to
- Researcher sign-off obtained on 5.0 (offered 4.5/5.0/6.0) before
  locking in, same process as the original significance_threshold_sigma
  decision
- single_band_significance_threshold_sigma: 5.0 (config); new
  qc_single_band_candidate block in assemble_level_b1, OR-across-bands
  (not AND like the dual-band criterion -- structurally correct since a
  single-filter-detection star only ever has one band with real
  photometry). Kept as its own flag, never folded into
  qc_candidate_preliminary

tests/test_excess.py:
- 5 new tests, including one specifically constructed to prove the
  "genuinely separate, stricter, not the primary threshold scaled down"
  requirement: a single-band star at sigma~4.0 clears the 3.0 dual-band
  bar but correctly fails the 5.0 single-band bar. Another confirms a
  dual-band star with one huge-sigma band does not leak into
  qc_single_band_candidate

Real-data verification (not just synthetic unit tests): re-ran
assemble_level_b1 against the real cached a0/a1/miri_photometry outputs
for all 15 trial-batch fields (273 stars) with the new config --
qc_single_band_candidate totals 0, qc_candidate_preliminary totals 0,
exactly the "changes zero current results" prediction, now confirmed
against real data.
