docs: full investigation of the two unresolved qc_candidate_preliminary sources (J1757132, AX-J1600.9-5142) -- external cross-match, pixel-level background checks, 8-band SED, literature-checked retraction

Investigation-only session, no pipeline code changed -- everything below
is logged as dated RESEARCH_CONTEXT.md Decision Log entries (2026-07-24),
with real numbers throughout, not summarized after the fact.

External cross-match (SIMBAD, AllWISE, ZTF), live queries against real
archive data for both stars' Gaia-DR3 positions:
- Decided the WISE-null interpretation before running any query: a
  non-detection is inconclusive given WISE's coarser PSF/shallower
  sensitivity vs. MIRI, not evidence against a real excess -- only
  informative if the implied flux limit sits below what the star's own
  MIRI flux requires
- J1757132: real AllWISE counterpart at 0.086", detected in W1/W2
  (normal colors) but W3/W4 are formal non-detections (SNR~0, qph=UU).
  Converted the W3 limit to a flux bound (110 uJy) and compared directly
  against this project's own EE-corrected F1000W flux (89 uJy, same
  APCORR-table method as miri_photometry.py) -- the WISE limit sits only
  ~1.24x above the real flux, i.e. genuinely sensitivity-limited, not
  contradictory. ZTF: 1300+ epochs, chi2~1.0-1.2, no variability --
  rules out an eclipsing/eruptive contaminant, not a static disk
- AX-J1600.9-5142: zero AllWISE sources within 6" including W1/W2, where
  a star this bright should be trivial to detect -- widened to 20" and
  found two bright (W1~8.6 mag), AllWISE-flagged-extended sources 11-12"
  away, confirming this is a confusion null, not a clean one. SIMBAD
  (60" search) independently corroborates a genuinely dusty field: a
  cataloged dark nebula, an open cluster, and the actual named WR star
  the field was pointed at, all within ~45-58". Zero ZTF coverage --
  Dec=-51.7 is outside ZTF's Palomar-based footprint, correctly treated
  as inconclusive by construction, not a real non-detection

Pixel-level local-background-annulus check (Deferred item 6), same
CRDS APCORR-table geometry the production pipeline uses, real
_i2d mosaics for both stars:
- J1757132 (F770W and F1000W both checked): annulus pixel scatter at or
  below photon-noise expectation in both bands; visual cutout confirms
  clean, uniform background. Worst-case sensitivity bound (faint-half
  vs. bright-half of the annulus as background) is +/-3% to +/-10%,
  well under the measured dual-band excess
- AX-J1600.9-5142 (F770W): annulus scatter is ~12.6x photon noise --
  real, not marginal -- with a clear monotonic azimuthal gradient
  (confirmed visually), not a radial one. Same-day follow-up: actually
  fit a least-squares plane to the annulus (the natural first version of
  a principled gradient correction) rather than just proposing one --
  result: the corrected background (43.625) is statistically identical
  to the pipeline's own sigma-clipped estimate (43.626), because a
  circular annulus centered on the star is provably immune to first-order
  (or any smooth-polynomial-order) gradient bias at its own center. This
  is a real correction, not a directional argument -- it means the
  original "quarter to a third of the excess" bound was a correctly-
  computed worst-case sensitivity ceiling, not a best estimate, and is
  walked back explicitly as a same-day correction (not silently revised).
  Also ruled out a mosaic exposure-coverage (WHT) artifact as the
  gradient's cause (r=0.167 correlation with SCI, different angular
  pattern) -- the diffuse structure is real signal, just not one that
  biases this star's specific, well-centered measurement

J1757132: real, already-public 8-filter MIRI archive data found for this
exact pointing (F560W/F1130W/F1280W/F1500W/F1800W/F2100W, alongside the
already-used F770W/F1000W) while chasing whether a better W3/W4
constraint than AllWISE exists (NEOWISE-R confirmed W1/W2-only, no help;
no deeper W3/W4 coadd exists anywhere). Extracted PSF photometry at all
8 filters using the exact production code
(miri_photometry.extract_flux_for_filter, imported directly), reproduced
the star's Kurucz fit exactly (Teff=3601.8 K, chi2=3.17, matching the
already-logged values) to validate the extension before trusting it:
- Result: NOT a smooth excess. Significant excess at 5.6-10 um (up to
  +15.6 sigma) flips to significant deficit at 11.3-21 um (down to
  -8.9 sigma), crossing near 11 um
- Literature check on the leading hypothesis (Kurucz/ATLAS9 molecular-
  opacity gap) before it could reach a public document: the general
  claim holds (well-documented unsuitability below ~3500-4000 K), but
  the one quantitative wavelength-resolved comparison found in the
  literature (a cool-giant study, wrong population for this log_g=4.5
  dwarf) predicts the wrong sign at the one wavelength it's specific
  about -- retracted as a named mechanism per instruction, reverted to
  "unexplained spectral shape"
- Found and disclosed a real, previously-invisible caveat while building
  the SED figure: the Kurucz ck04models grid's native wavelength sampling
  is dense only to ~10 um, then has a single point at 20 um and nothing
  in between -- meaning every predicted_flux value from F1130W-F1800W
  (not just this star, any future use) rests on interpolation across
  that gap, not resolved model structure
- Final, decisive test (external review round): does the excess/deficit
  crossover survive an independent, gap-free prediction? Refit with
  PHOENIX (reproducing the already-logged 3600 K, chi2=9.9 cross-check)
  and the pipeline's own Rayleigh-Jeans extrapolation (extended past its
  stock 15 um ceiling for this test only, same anchor logic, not a
  pipeline edit) -- the crossover does not survive: replaced by a roughly
  flat ~9-21% deficit, no sign flip. The two models' own predictions
  differ in shape by up to 79% relative to each other across the band --
  enough alone to produce or erase a crossover depending on which is
  used. Downgraded fully per instruction: most likely a model-comparison
  artifact, not real astrophysics; the milder uniform deficit that
  survives both models is reported as real but far less dramatic, and
  itself degenerate with PHOENIX's weaker fit to the star's real
  photometry
- Checked for a mundane instrumental alternative before finalizing:
  MIRI's documented time-dependent chromatic flux-calibration systematic
  (3-12% bias, right direction) -- this data's Dec-2025 processing date
  and CRDS reference-file version, cross-checked against the current
  calibration paper's sub-1% post-correction uncertainty, make this
  unlikely to dominate but it was not independently re-derived for this
  star. Separately flagged, not omitted: F560W/F770W's documented
  "cruciform" PSF-modeling incompleteness (~3%) at exactly the two
  filters showing the largest apparent excess

AX-J1600.9-5142: reframed honestly rather than left as a symmetric open
case -- real diffuse background (13x photon noise), a cataloged dark
nebula and WR star within 1 arcmin, and a confusing WISE neighbor all
point toward PSF-wing leakage as the most probable explanation, without
claiming the PSF-subtraction/deblending modeling that would confirm it
was actually done (it wasn't; not yet built into this pipeline).

Cross-checked a standing methodological concern against real data rather
than reasoning from commit dates alone: verified neither flagged source's
Gaia match was a casualty of the 2026-07-15 row-misalignment bug by
comparing each star's Gaia position directly against its own real,
independently-fitted MIRI PSF centroid (0.23" and 0.10" separation,
both well inside the pipeline's 0.25" crossmatch radius) -- confirmed
clean.
